]]> The redesign operations for any site starts with a lot of planning and strategizing. But more often than not, these practices do not bring in expected results. What might be the reason for that?

The main problem is the fact that most of the time clients come in with the idea that web design and web redesign are the same processes. The familiarity with one assures them of familiarity with another process, and that produces problems.

There is a sharp variation within website redesign challenge and normal design challenges. 

Both procedures are very contrasting from the inside. And that’s why I with my team have created this guide that intends to clear out the confusion and provide an in-depth understanding of how to redesign a website.

Take Stock Of Your Website

This is the thing about redesigning your website–  it starts with an already existing and functional site.

It is not a ‘build-from-the-scratch’ mission, rather ‘fix-the-problematic-area’ mission. And to do it right, the site has to be inspected first. And that’s why this is the first step of this website redesign guide.

You’ll find plenty of issues when inspecting the site. These areas undoubtedly need to be fixed. Now based on the website, you’ll find various issues. 

During my experience in running a web design company in Casablanca, I have gathered information that can help you make the decision the right way.

Here is a list of some basic complications that happen to every site. 

Pro Tip: There are plenty of good analytics tools out there like Google Analytics,  Google Pagespeed Insights, GTmatrix, etc. Use these to get a full idea of the state of the website.

A. Lack Of Parallelity 

Every business has a unique identity. And the design needs to reflect it seamlessly. A lot of business owners looking for affordable website design forget to count in the matter of business identity.

And then disaster ensues.

Even though initially your site matched with business identity, later on, it might not. The identity of the business can change as it grows, and the site design needs to change accordingly as well. 

So take a look at the site and consider if it matches the business identity. If it doesn’t, then it is time to change it. 

B. Where Are The Results?

There are plenty of reasons for a business to have a site. From escalating revenue generation to getting traffics. But at the end of the day, only one goal matters, and that is- to make the business more successful. 

So when analyzing the old website, ask if the website bringing in the kind of result expected? If not, then put the site through inspection and pinpoint the problem.

This is going to be an important part of the entire website redesign strategy.

C. The Relevancy Of The Design

Web design is evolving every day. The modern website design trends change regularly, and most successful ones out there are keeping up without any problem. 

If the site was designed in early 2010 and carrying the same design since then, it’s high time for a redesign. Backdated designs cause both aesthetic issues and technical issues, rendering the site unusable. 

D. Issue With Engagement

A good website is always appealing to its users.

When considering a website redesign think about whether the website is attracting the users. Inspect the web content, images, information structure of the website. If it is failing to engage and excite the visitors, then it is time to change it all.

E. The Bounce Rate Dilemma 

This issue puts a lot of sites in jeopardy. The more people leave as soon as they enter the site, the bigger problem will be.  

There can be plenty of reasons for users to desert the site. These reasons can range from misleading page content to slow website.

Whatever it might be, the website analysis stage can determine these reasons and help in finding solutions. 

Outline The Website Redesign You Want

Website redesign fixes the problems of the site as well as adds more elements to it. This is the time to consider the ingredients that make a perfect site is like and include those elements on the website.

The process won’t work without a clear action plan. After acquiring the basic idea of what needs to be changed, go on and list out what needs to be added to the websites.

The requirements might be different based on the type of trade. but the basic requirements for a successful website remain the same. And if the website is lacking any of these, then add it to the list of requirements ASAP.

Pro Tip: For the website redesign process, conduct a market and competitor inspection. Analyze the prosperous websites in the industry, and compare that to the website. Find out what is lacking, and add those requirements to the list.

A. Well Planned Page Architecture

Every page needs to follow an architectural pattern necessary to help site visitors find the right information. This architecture is based on a lot of research and visitor behavior on the page. 

Hiring a website redesign company for this matter is going to be the best way to proceed. With proper help, it will be easier to understand how the demographic has changed and what kind of IA they prefer on web pages.

The structure of the information on the page can always be improved for better results.  

B. Website Navigation: The Smoother The Better

This element is important to provide users with a clear way of finding information on the site.

Done perfectly, it also assists the site to perform well among the search results. It works with the user intuition to provide them with a streamlined path to information on the site. 

There’s always room for improvement, and even if the website has a functional navigational flow, it can be improved upon. While analyzing, compare the entire flow of wayfinding on the site with other sites in the industry.

This comparison will help to understand what is lacking in the site’s navigation. This is going to be a much-needed addition to your website redesign strategy.

C. Does Your Website Have The Client’s Trust?

No business functions without having their client’s trust. And when it comes to earning the trust of the clients, content is a key player. 

The content of the website assists in convincing the people about the quality and authenticity of the work. The redesign is going to be the perfect time to do a content analysis, and then change them up to gain the client’s trust. Good quality content is necessary for earning the client’s trust in business. 

D. Conversion funnel (including placements of CTAs)

Every site exists to alter visitors into clients. There is a clear flow of path to this transformation and it is visualized as a funnel, and hence why the name Conversion funnel.

There are four steps to this- awareness, interest, design, and action. It is an automated system designed to attract and transform potential clients. 

There’s no need for any manual action if the site already consists of a clear path of transformation for clients. The website redesign process is the perfect time to understand how to integrate this automated system to get, maximum clients. 

The Action Stage

Nothing changes without action. Even the previous two steps, necessary for the redesign would not work if the right actions are not taken.

A lot of times problems appear at this stage due to the lack of knowledge on how to proceed through this project. 

Here is a list of actions you need to take in order to ensure that the redesign process becomes successful.

A. Choosing The Right Team to Plan Your Website Redesign

Having the right team for the website redesigning process is absolutely crucial. Whether it is an in house designers and developers, of hiring an agency, the team needs to have the right players. 

Here is a list of professional website designers and other professionals needed for the entire project-

  • Digital strategist: this is the member of the team who will define the whole strategy to transform visitors for the redesigned site. 
  • Information architect: the team member who will build the right structure to present information to the end-user as well as build an overall structure to help the user find the way through the website.
  • UX designer: this person will take all the input from the above-mentioned people and then create a site that is designed for maximum levels of efficiency on the website. 

A lot of clients come in thinking that only a designer is needed for the redesign. The fact remains that it is the very last task. The above team members will conduct crucial tasks, such as market analysis, brand study, etc. The designer will build based on this research. 

Pro Tip: Even if a website redesign agency claims to include professionals, do not believe them. Check out the portfolio and client review of that agency prior to making any decisions on hiring.

B. Aim High With Proper Planning for Website Redesign

Every site is built with different objectives in mind that are subjected to change over time. And the site needs to reflect this change perfectly. 

During the redesign process, it is necessary to evaluate the previous goals and then change them when necessary. The changes occur depending on client demographic, changing industry scenarios, and the size of the business.

It’s crucial to consult local website designer before redefining the objectives and diving deeper into the process. 

C. A Fictional Persona To Understand The Requirments

Once the goals are set, it’s time for you and the website redesign services you’ve hired to think and imagine a client persona. This is essential for understanding the demographic, its needs, and how to best cater to it. 

Here is a list of information required for this step-

  1. Name
  2. Age 
  3. Address, 
  4. Reasons for accessing the internet
  5. How they access the internet
  6. What devices are used 
  7. Behavior on the internet (including the type of websites they search for, the kind of things they buy, etc)
  8. Their needs, frustration, and motivation

This stage assists with redesigning the site interface. This ensures a memorable encounter with the site for the visitors and makes them come back again and again. 

D. Time To Finalize The Product

The final step is the culmination of all the research and tasks you’ve done till now. All the results and statistics collected go into the design phase, which can be divided into a few steps. 

The design phase is divided into four parts. And sure these often overlap but having clear and precise knowledge can ensure that the website redesign process ends successfully. 

  • Wireframe creation

This is the first stage. It is when the skeleton frame of the site is built, depending on the information on IA, navigations best practices, etc.  

  • Content creation

Once the wireframing is done, comes the content creation stage. This stage is based heavily on the research and analysis data from previous stages. All content is aimed at impressing and gaining the trust of the visitors. 

  • Designing the UI

At this stage, the interface of the site is revamped. Using the research information and modern website design trends, the site interface is updated, then handed over to the developers for development.

  • Development 

This is the last point. Once the site is finalized through coding and programming, it goes in for a final check for bugs and other problems. Once these are smoothened out, the site can go live for the users. 

And that’s about it, the entire procedure of reconstructing the design. But before concluding, here is an extra advice section- the errors to dodge during this process.

Mistakes To Avoid At All Costs

When it comes to redesigning your website there are a few errors that can be extremely harmful. Keep an eye open throughout the procedure so that none of these errors happen to the site.

A. Not Testing The Website Before Launch

Not testing the site before launching is a grave error to make. Once the site is done developing, put it through the testing procedure in order to determine that it reaches all the necessary goals. 

B. Not Integrating A Sitemap

The sitemap of a site helps the bots to scan it. It is important to improve the navigation of the site, as well as for improving site rankings. 

C. Spending Budget Yet Unnecessary Integrations

The ordinary misconception about the entire operation often leads the clientele to blow the budget over unnecessary integrations on the site believing that it is going to help it to be successful. However, this only increases the web redesign cost and produces problems. So frame the budget carefully before starting the operation.

Wrapping Up

Different kinds of clients perceive web redesign in different ways. Some consider it to be incredibly daunting, while some think of it in a lightweight manner. No matter what, the right moves in redesign operation is crucial for the success of the site. ]]> So, you’ve just launched a brand new website for your business and expecting loads of online clients. All in all, you’re very excited!

However, even after enough time, there is no business from your website. There are no clients coming in, and the bounce rate is sky-high even after trying out all the web page tricks. And you kind of feel like a kid being handed socks as presents on Christmas morning…

Now, before you compile the best in class strategists to find out where you went wrong with the website strategy, hear us out— it’s probably the design that crushed all your dreams of getting high numbers of clients from the website. 

It might seem fake, but it’s true. When browsing for any product, most site visitors are going to check out the design of your website before they check the content to determine if your product is credible enough. If you can’t impress them with the design then forget about impressing them with the content. 

So, what should you do in this situation? Well, the most obvious solution is the website redesign. Partially or completely overhauling the website design to make it user-friendly can go a long way to ensure the success of the website. Now if you are thinking about how to redesign a website, then you’re at the right place, cause we are going to tell you about all the steps you need to follow to redesign your website for better.

The Steps To Be Taken: For The Redesigning Of Your Website

Before we dive into the details on how to redesign your website to answer all your web redesign questions, let’s have a quick look at all the steps you need to take to redesign your website-

  1. Analyze your old website design
  2. Find ELTWO: Elements that work
  3. Make proper use of your user data
  4. Build your idea wireframes
  5. List the elements that need a redesign

Now that we have the web redesign checklist, let’s go through them and see what they entail.

Step By Step Process Of Redesigning Your Personal Website

The process of redesigning your personal website is pretty simple. It’s all about recognizing the problem areas of the design of your website and then designing them again to match the requirements of your business.

However, before you hire website redesign services, you have to understand the steps, and below, we have discussed each of them in detail. 

1. Before Changing The Old, Let’s Analyze It

Before you begin the website redesign process, stop and look at the already established design you have on your website. This will help you to design the website design strategy template to draw up the web redesign proposal. 

It is important for you to analyze the old design because you have to circle out the flaws of the old design to change them. Redesigning the website without analyzing the old design will only create more problems. Before understanding the website redesign requirements, you have to understand why you need redesign in the first place. 

If you are asking why to redesign a website, then you need to analyze the old design of the text. Pretty sure you will find many reasons to redesign your website Here are a few of those reasons-

A: The Design Is Ages Old

The first reason why you would need to redesign your website is the age of the design. We all know that fine wine is something that ages well, sadly it is not the same when it comes to website design. The design of a website needs to be updated with time, adapting to the latest design trends and techniques. Failing to do so will make your website look positively medieval, and that is not going to be appealing to the website visitors. 

B: Designing Better UX

The second reason why you might have to redesign your website is to give the users a better UX. if the UX of the website is not up to par, you might receive complaints, or maybe have the bounce rate speak on behalf of the users. In any case, designing a better UX can be one of the main reasons why you might find yourself having to go through the web revamp process. 

C: Integration Of Latest Tools

Technology is advancing by the minutes. And that’s why we have more tools available for website designing today than we had last year. And it goes without saying that to leverage the best of these tools, you have to redesign your website to match their technical requirements. 

2. Finding ELTWO: Elements That Work

Just because your website needs redesigning, does not mean everything needs to be changed. In fact, you will find that many aspects of your website work perfectly fine. And if they do work fine, then there’s no need for any web design changes. 

These elements can include the logo, images, typography, or the colors used on the website, anything. When analyzing the old design of your website, if you happen to find any elements of the design that works perfectly fine, you should integrate them into your website seamlessly. 

3. Make Use Of The User Data

Understanding the user from the very beginning of the website design process should’ve been a priority for you. But if you failed to apply it, then no worries, you can do it during the redesign. 

You will collect plenty of data throughout the operation period to analyze and learn what exactly went wrong with the design of your website in the first place. This user data will answer all your questions about where the users got stuck while navigating the page, where they got exhausted and left the page altogether. It will help you recognize all the snags in the design and smooth it out. 

4. Build Wireframes

How about before you redesign and launch it, you build an interactive MVP or wireframe, and have it tested?

It will help you to have built a wireframe first and have it tested before actually implementing the design. You can collect data on how hard or easy it was for the users to navigate through the entire design, the points they got stuck on and the navigational points they were confused about. Creating a wireframe before redesigning is going to only enhance the benefits of the redesign process. 

5. List The Elements That Need Redesign

Now we come to the most important part- the design. 

Of course, when talking about website redesign we can not skip over the part of the design. There are many elements of designing you have to take care of while redesigning. Below are a few of them.

A: Make It Responsive

The first design element you have to consider is the responsiveness of the website. You need to keep in mind that today most people will be using their phones to access your website. And in order to accommodate them, you have to make sure that your website is responsive. 

Fortunately, it doesn’t take a lot of effort to turn your website into a responsive website and helps a lot to impress and convert clients. So if your website is not already mobile-friendly, then make sure that it is during the redesign phase. 

B: Using Proper Images And Illustrations

We have briefly talked about the importance of visual design, but for this point, we have to talk about it in detail. 

The visual impact of the website design is more important than you think. Apparently, 46.1% of visitors on a website decide on the credibility of the website after looking at the design. This is why to make the most of the visual impact, you have to use authentic images and illustrations that are relevant to your product. 

It is an easier and perhaps even cheaper solution to use stock images and illustrations you might find online. But, they are not going to help with the credibility of the website. Even though these photographs are of high quality, they still seem fake, and you can not increase consumer trust in your brand with fake images. 

The use of authentic images will help users to build up trust in you. So, conduct a photoshoot, or have your hired designers from website redesigning company design up some original and unique illustrations. Sure it might be a little costly and time consuming, but the positive effect of it will be worth it. 

C: Branding Through Colors And Logo

When analyzing your previous design, make sure to check if the entire aesthetic of the website, including texts and typography, colors and logo are matching your brand value. Is it how you want your business to be known? If not, then you have to opt for a complete redesign for your website to reflect the brand of your business.

Among the many aspects that’ll affect the brand image of your website are color and logo. In the long haul, users are going to remember you by the colors you used and the logo of your business. For maximum effect, you have to place the logo on the top left corner of the page. That is where the user is going to look first to find the logo. 

Analyzing the famous brands in the market will help us better understand the importance of color in branding. Think of Facebook, what color do you see? Is it blue? That’s because from the beginning facebook has used the color blue to present themselves as a calm, secure and steadfast social media platform. The color of your website should reflect the nature of your business. 

When rebranding becomes necessary during redesigning, ensure that the color you choose for your brand reflects the value of what your business does, along with invoking a positive emotion among them. This simple decision will go a long way to make your brand a world-famous one. 

D: Adding Landing Pages

If the conversion is one of the main website redesign objectives for your website, then you have to make sure to create new landing pages. The landing pages are going to be focused on converting the site visitors through discounts, offers, or email subscriptions list. The conversion rate of your website is definitely going to rise with the right landing pages. 

Once you have the proper landing pages in place, analyze the user response to them. When you run website optimization reports, you will get a whole lot of insight into how the user is reacting to the page you have designed. And based on that insight and web page design ideas and tips you can later change the design to match their requirements.

Best Practices That We Follow

To deliver the best product in the market, you have to follow some best practices in the market. And throughout the years we have gathered enough experience to create a list of best practices we follow like the sacred texts. What are these best practices you ask? Well, let’s see-

A: We Know The Secret Language Of Design

Designing a website is all about earning the trust of the user through the psychological influences of the design principles. And when it comes to following the web design principles popular in Casablanca and other areas, no one can do it better than us.

When we talk about navigation, we don’t mean navigation of a ship, but the navigation of a website. Presenting the users with so many options will only make them confused and dazed. And that’s why we follow the Hick-Hyman Law.
