2. Predictive Analytics – To Know Your Customer Better

Well, it has been in the market for quite a while now. However, generally, the take up has been a bit low. Maybe due to inhibitive costs and complexity associated with it. 

However, with Big Data in the market, trends are rapidly changing. Now, you will find plenty of less complicated and more affordable solutions available. 

Predictive analytics encompasses a variety of statistical techniques. The techniques analyse historical and current data. And generate predictions about upcoming events. 

With Predictive analytics, your eCommerce businesses can

  • Know in advance what your customers are most likely to buy
  • Determine the maximum price a customer is ready to pay for your product
  • Practice better price management
  • Target promotions and recommendations
  • Enhance business intelligence
  • Reduce fraud
  • Improve supply chain management

So, to make the most money on your sales, using predictive analytics is crucial for your eCommerce business growth in 2020.

3. Artificial Intelligence – To Offer Better Shopping Experience

Today, Artificial Intelligence (AI) is everywhere. According to Gartner’s prediction, by 2020, AI will handle over 80% of all customer interactions.

eCommerce giants like eBay, Amazon, Alibaba, Rakuten, etc. are already reaping the benefits of AI in eCommerce. So, not using AI in your business is only letting your competitors beat you! 

Benefits of Artificial Intelligence in Your eCommerce Business

Usually, the main objectives of an eCommerce business are as follows.

  • To understand customers better
  • Generate new leads
  • To provide an enhanced user experience

Now, with AI implemented in your online business, you can

  • Offer customer-centric visual search
  • Retarget potential customers
  • Improve the sales process
  • Approach customers with more personalized tone
  • Improve recommendations for customers
  • Provide intelligent shopping assistants
  • Offer automation
  • Filter fake reviews, etc.

AI is changing the way we sell and buy online. In the coming years and beyond, we believe AI will impact a lot. From customer retention to satisfaction, transactions to business efficiency, and a lot. So, integrating AI in a must-include for eCommerce 2019 trends. 

4. Voice Search – To Assist Your Customers Grab The Best Deal

Well, experts believe that voice search is the next big thing in the eCommerce landscape.

This technology has an accuracy rate of 95%. Already products like Google Assistant, Amazon Alexa, Siri, and Microsoft Cortana are trending in the market. In fact, from the past few years, voice-empowered shopping has been one of the most discussed technology trends in the eCommerce industry. 

The benefits you can avail using voice search in eCommerce are

  • Better customer experience
  • Better store navigation
  • Increased mobile purchases
  • Secure payment gateways

Thus, the next logical step becomes to integrate voice search into your eCommerce web design trends. 

5. Social Selling – To Reach Broader Audience

Today, considering the power of social networking in eCommerce is no more surprising! Most of the social media big players are offering some kind of eCommerce integration. 

You can see that Twitter is launching product pages. Instagram added a “shop now” button. Facebook now allows brands to use a “buy” button. 

In Q3 2017, Facebook earned around $10.14 billion in advertising revenue alone. No wonder why brands see social media networks as a huge pool of potential customers today. 

How social media platforms attract brands? They offer different user targeting options to businesses. Also, if managed effectively, they offer businesses

  • High levels of customization and consumer reach
  • Profitable conversion rates
  • Detailed reporting, and
  • High engagement rates

Usually, while scrolling through their feeds, customers have to leave the page to make a purchase. It creates friction in the buying process. You have to overcome this friction.

You can allow customers to click or swipe on your product straight from the feeds. And allow them to pay instantly. Thus, using social selling, you can increase the rates of impulse buys.

Consistent social selling increases sales goals by 40% than non-social sellers. Also, it generates leads and creates a deeper relationship with customers. 

It is expected that by 2020, more brands will understand the importance of social selling. Just running Google Shopping ads is not enough anymore! So, make sure you are connecting sales to your social media account.

6. Holy Grail of Growth – Personalization

Personalization is about creating a personal experience for each consumer. It is usually done through tailored recommendations. Search queries, purchase history, geolocation, and browsing history are some of the aspects based on which tailored recommendation is done. 

Well, let’s take a look at some of the surprising stats on personalization.

  • 74% of customers feel frustrated when website content is not personalized.
  • 81% of consumers want brands to get to know them. They expect brands to understand when to approach them and when not to.
  • 75% of consumers are more likely to buy from a retailer that recognizes them by name. Recommends options based on past purchases. Or knows their purchase history. 

No wonder why personalization increases businesses’ profits by 15%. 

So, want to shine in eCommerce business? Embrace more personalization while approaching your customers.

7. Chatbots – The Ultimate Shopping Guide For Your Customer

We already mentioned the role of Artificial Intelligence in the eCommerce business. Now, one of its successful implementations is chatbots. The successful implementation of chatbots makes your eCommerce business way smarter than your competitors. 

Chatbots not only save time but also provide automated and fast answers to customers’ queries. Instead of waiting days for an assistant to call, customers get instant answers. Bei it early morning or midnight, customers never have to wait for answers. It undoubtedly offers great customer satisfaction. Thus, your business’s customer service reaches a new height! 

Also, integrating chatbots in your eCommerce trends 2019-2020 can save a lot of operational costs. How? Well, investing once in the chatbot integration is cheaper than hiring a dedicated worker. Businesses do not have to pay for overtime, sick time or vacation time then. 

So, if you already have not planned for it, it is high time to do so. Implement chatbots in your online store. And enjoy great ROI by improving your customer service and 24X7 availability. 

8. Conversion Optimization – Product Page Vs Home Page

Usually, businesses have an obsession to design intriguing home pages. However, especially for eCommerce websites, putting a bit more emphasis on the internal pages can do wonders.

The reason is quite simple. The homepage is more often seen by repeated visitors. Either they search your brand name in Google. Or they go directly to your website. The new visitors tend to land on internal service or product pages more frequently. Also, this is probably their first interaction with your brand. Hence, an impressive internal page design may turn your new visitors into regular customers. 

Put yourself in the customers’ shoes. And design the product pages in the way your first-time visitors want to see it. A stellar first impression is crucial for increasing the conversion rate. Hence, put more efforts to optimize your internal product page designs. Thus, this is definitely going to be a major part of the eCommerce web design trends 2019 – 2020. 

9. The New Normal – Mobile Payments

This is no more a trend. But, mobile payments have become a necessity for today’s eCommerce businesses. According to a Statista report, by 2021, 53.9% of all retail eCommerce is expected to be generated through mCommerce. 

Well, the rise of mCommerce is no more surprising. With the growing trend of smartphone usage and eCommerce mobile apps, we all knew this had to happen someday! 

Today, we all are aware of digital wallets like PayPal, Google Pay, Samsung Pay or Apple Pay. Worldwide eCommerce businesses are widely implementing these digital wallets. All these digital services allow users to make purchases through electronic transactions. Undoubtedly, it allows more frictionless shopping experiences for the customers. Also, a market survey reported that 70% of people expect that by 2030, digital payments will overtake cards and cash. 

Recently, cryptocurrency is also cooking up a storm. Though we have not seen it being implemented by eCommerce businesses so far. Still, we can expect things can go this way soon! Well, definitely, maybe! 

10. Subscription-based Business Models

In the U.S. alone, 5.7 million subscription box shoppers are there! No doubt why subscription-based business models have become the new trend. 

Subscription-based eCommerce has gained a lot of traction. The two main reasons for the demand for subscription-based eCommerce are as follows.

  • Ease of automated monthly direct debit.
  • The excitement of monthly products delivered.

Product subscription boxes are becoming fun these days. From makeup to pet products and from food boxes to shavers. The subscription-based eCommerce entices customers by offering personalized boxes right to their doors. 

So, introducing a subscription element into your eCommerce business can be a game-changer! 

11. Bonus: eCommerce Web Design Trends

So far, you saw the major eCommerce website development trends in 2020. Now, we are going to talk about some eCommerce web design trends. 

On the internet, a site is a business’s storefront. Especially for eCommerce businesses, it is crucial to attracting prospective customers. And ultimately turning them into paying customers. Just like a brick and mortar store, the designs and layouts of an online store play a game-changing role here. It can be the difference between turning a prospect into a potential customer. Or bouncing away in favor of a competitor! 

Digital experience can dictate the entire experience a customer has with a business. So, you can afford to get left behind.

A. Mobile Era

A huge percentage of online shoppers prefer using mobiles to shop. So, designers have to create e-commerce websites for mobile-first. And then only adapt to larger screens. In the coming years, we will see more mobile-friendly features. Card-like layouts, finger-friendly icons, and long scrolling pages will be seen on most of the eCommerce website themes. 

B. Thumb Navigation for Mobile

As continued from the above point, the next eCommerce web design trend is obvious! Statistics show that 94% of smartphone users hold their devices vertically. Hence, to facilitate thumb use, positioning the navigation buttons at the screen bottom will be a good practice. 

C. Striking Colors

Well, the use of white space and minimalistic design are already trending in the market. However, this year, we are probably going to see the experimental use of striking and bold colors in eCommerce web design trends 2019-2020. For eCommerce websites, the monochromatic design is not very appropriate. 

D. Vintage Photography

Reviving the vintage styles is one of the most trending eCommerce trends in recent days. In vintage photography, we can see the use of a muted color palette. This is reminiscent of the gritty, low-fi photography of the 70s. Also, colors are here imbued with more black than other modern styles. It heightens that dated look.

So, other than all these, you have to make sure your eCommerce site design implements the following points. 

  • Micro-interactions
  • More and more video content
  • Contextual logos
  • Storytelling approach, etc.

]]> Do you know, if you do not have a smooth payment gateway, then it can drive your buyers away? The complex payment gateway asking too many details about customer puts off anybody who comes to your website, shopping for his or her favourite product. If you are planning to start your online store, then keep in mind that a payment gateway is one of the most important areas where you should concentrate. Make the payment process hassle free, so that customers don’t have a tiring experience while making the payment.

Any e-commerce web development company wants a good reputation among its customers. A smooth payment gateway is vital for any business that wants to stay longer in the market. UIPL recommends six types of payment gateways that will help you to decide the best payment gateway for your e-commerce website.

Hosted Payment Gateways

Here, the customer is redirected to a third party website of the payment service provider once he or she clicks the ‘Pay Now’ button. He needs to fill in important details such as ‘Name on card,’ ‘Expiry Date,’ ‘CVV.’ Once he fills the details, a One Time Password (OTP) is sent to the customer, once this process is done, the customer will again be redirected to that particular online shopping website.

iFrame or inline Frame is another payment gateway. Here, a link is created by the payment service provider to the merchants. The customers do not have to insert any details. All they have to do is fill up a form and the PSP automatically transfers the data. In case, the customer has any outstanding payments to make, the PSP creates a profile of the specific customer and goes on deducting the recurring amount while sending notifications to the website from where he or she purchased the product.

Benefits to the merchant

1. All details of the cardholder are captured by PSP

2. The logo of the merchant’s business can be attached in the payment page

3. The PSP takes care of the payment set up

Self Hosted Payment Gateways

Here, the customer is not redirected to any third party website. All the details are taken by the e-commerce website itself. After this, the data is sent to the URL of the payment gateway. This is done by either sending the data in a specific format or by sending any secret code. Any outstanding payment from the customer’s side is debited by the payment gateway. As soon as this is done, a notification is sent to the e-commerce website.

Benefits to the merchant

1. the way a customer navigates through the website till the checkout process in order to get an idea about the things that a customer looks for when he or she visits a website for the first time.

2. Get insight about the customer’s shopping preference.

Non-Hosted Payment Gateways

If you want your customers to enter their details in your website, then Non-Hosted Payment Gateways are something, which you should opt for. Also known as API, here any information of the customer related to the transaction can be accessed on the web page itself. This is done with the help of API. Based on the information provided by the customer regarding his payment details, payment calls are made after which notifications are sent to the website.

Benefits to the merchants:

1. View the User Interface of the payment page.

2. Enhanced customer experience.

3. Connect internet payment solution with mobile phones, tablets or any kind of technological device that can access an e-commerce website.

Direct Payment Gateway

There are many payment processors that do not give instant payment notification. The amount is debited from the user’s debit or credit card without informing the system. Only a notification of the credit card’s approval is generated. In this situation, the system constantly updates the payment processor whether the payment is received or not.

Benefits to the merchants:

The user is not redirected to any payment gateway.

Platform Based Payment Gateway Solutions

This is an excellent platform for selling your goods directly from the server itself. After the users have selected the items they want to purchase, customers need to subscribe themselves first and then they are redirected to the checkout button. The purchase can be completed in any language or currency.

Benefits to the merchants:

1. Improve the conversion of buyers.

2. Shows the price as well as currency of the shopper. You will get an idea about the geographic location of your buyers.

3. Receive foreign payments according to the latest exchange rates.

The above mentioned payment gateways will help you to decide which is the one that suits your business need, but before deciding the payment gateways there are a number of factors that you have to keep in mind.

1. Know the transaction fees charged by the payment gateway.

2. Do not opt for any kind of payment gateways that asks you to register yourself.

3. Always choose a payment gateway that accepts multiple currencies. This is applicable only when your business is spread in more than one country.

4. Read the terms and conditions of the payment gateway carefully. Check what type of products you can sell.

5. Check and make sure the payment gateway you have selected does not ask people to fill a plenty of personal details. This will certainly drive away visitors from the website.

6. Your payment gateway must accept Credit Card, Debit Card and Paypal payments.

A payment gateway plays a vital role in any business. The customers must rely on you when it comes to payment. At the same time, a safe and secure payment process will always make your customers coming back to your website. The concept of e-commerce is developing day by day and in the near future it is expected to increase. With the growth of internet penetration and tech-savvy population all over the world, e-commerce will certainly flourish. Along with this, payment gateway will also gain much importance while creating an e-commerce website because this is one of the key factors for developing customer reputation.
