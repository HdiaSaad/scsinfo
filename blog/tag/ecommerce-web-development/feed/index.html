]]> Online shopping has become a common practice around the world. But the significance of ecommerce future trends have come to the forefront especially during the COVID-19 pandemic. When quarantine and work-from-home became the new normal in 2020, businesses and emerging ecommerce companies had no choice but to go digital. This has contributed to e-commerce’s share of global retail trade from 14% in 2019 to about 17% in 2020. (source: Unctad)

1. Taking Your E-Commerce Game Up a Notch

With new trends enhancing e-commerce, online customer experience (CX) and store engagement have become intimately tied to business outcomes. Customers have high standards and have become particular regarding their expectations from brands. So, if your website is not appealing, 38% of users will stop engaging with your content. (Source: Hubspot)

So, how should you build a robust website/apps that also converts?

We at SCS INFO have helped our clients to digitally transform themselves using the best practices while staying ahead of the competition. To know more, please see our ecommerce website development services (web/apps, saas development) – 1200+ solutions delivered for small and medium businesses, funded startups, enterprises, and Fortune 500 clients globally.

To augment your website’s UX+CX and achieve your business goals across all the stages of the customer journey i.e. from lead generation to conversion, customer engagement and loyalty, we have listed some b2b e-commerce trends:

  • AR to Boost Customer Experience (CX) – Digital platforms are using augmented reality to empower consumers’ shopping experience. Now, shoppers can get a preview of products and services in real-time through the ‘try on’ or ‘view’ feature. The beauty and cosmetic industry is leveraging the power of augmented reality (AR) to transform the e-shopping experience. With the help of AR, consumers can virtually full-fill makeup applications to mimic how they would try cosmetics on at a traditional brick and mortar store.
  • The Power of Artificial Intelligence (AI) – With the help of AI, companies can deliver hyper-personalized and enriching customer experiences.

Here are two of the most powerful AI powered ecommerce future trends that can skyrocket your top of the funnel (TOF):

  • Voice Commerce Powering CX – Why type when you could easily voice your query? If an e-commerce store does not have a digital assistant, this can be a major drop-off point for users. They will get tired of typing out their queries as it slows down their shopping experience. An Emarketer survey revealed that 40% of internet users in the US use voice search. Evidently, businesses can leverage the ‘voice-to-cart’ technology to boost a website’s engagement rate and sales.
  • Customer Service Chatbots – Customer assistance chatbots can really help to convert visitors into customers. Like in-store salespeople, chatbots help consumers complete their purchase and offer after-sales support. This is a way for businesses and emerging e-commerce companies to communicate with customers (including communication using regional languages) and resolve their issues then and there. In fact, 40% of millennials say that they use chatbots daily as they provide answers instantly. (Source: Slicktext)
  • Big Data for Personalized Experience– Online stores do not have retail salespeople to recommend products or answer shoppers’ queries. That is why it is crucial to have human touchpoints in a customer’s journey on e-commerce platforms. To mimic a user’s personalized in-store experience, online retailers are extracting customers’ information through their social media, browsing behavior, and prior buying patterns. Successful e-commerce brands are leveraging analytics to make UX data-driven and target consumers with exactly what they desire and even more.
  • Headless Commerce: A Solution to Increase Sales – Headless commerce is the separation of the front end i.e. the marketplace, user interface, graphics from the back-end that manages the product details and the portal’s entire functioning. This allows e-commerce website development companies being more creative, allowing freedom and flexibility to offer unique and personalized services to customers.

2. Conclusion: E-Commerce UX Trends Are Here to Stay

Emerging ecommerce future trends have gained a lot of traction in 2021 already, driving more traffic to businesses and converting them into potential customers. The pandemic has made us realize how powerful e-commerce is. In fact, it is predicted by Nasdaq that by 2040, 95% of purchases will be through e-commerce platforms.

3. Application And Modification Of The MVC Architecture

While the Magento architecture does follow the MVC pattern, it also adds some of its own modifications to it as well. 

A simple MVC structure divides up the codes into three parts- Model ( the main application), View (the reusable objects on the interface used by the Controller to perform tasks) and Controller ( how the model is presented to the user) 

When the Controller is asked to perform a task, it employes Model to process data and puts forth the results using the view. 

Magento, on the other hand, has added some additional sun-blocks to the traditional MVC structure. This modification helps Magento to support bigger eCommerce marketplace systems. 

As we can see from the image above, the Model in Magento’s MVC structure is divided into two parts and the View is divided into three structures. The helpers are in this case module-specific. This doesn’t only help the developers to manage a complex eCommerce system, but also helps the website to grow with the business. 

4. Marketplace Types

Before we start talking about the development and features, you need to choose what type of marketplace you want to build. 

When it comes to Magento marketplace development, there are two types you can go with. The first is a single vendor marketplace and the second is multi-vendor marketplaces. 

a. Single Vendor Marketplace

The single vendor marketplaces usually represent a website where a single company, sells its products. This can be either a B2B marketplace built using Magento or a simple B2C eCommerce website.  In this scenario, there are only two parties involved, the buyers and the sellers. These stand-alone websites will be perfect for you if you have a business that makes a specific brand of products and wants to sell that one particular website, avoiding competition. 

b. Multi-Vendor Marketplace

Multi-vendor marketplaces such as Amazon, or eBay have a number of vendors selling various products on one platform. In the case of a multi-vendor platform, we have three kinds of users, system admin, vendor, and the end-user or customer. 

The vendor sells products through its separate panels. The admin panel is used to manage the vendor and customer activity on the platform.

7. Magento Community vs Enterprise Edition: Which One To Choose?

Now that you have made the decision to build your eCommerce marketplace with Magento, you have to choose which version you want to use. 

When it comes to choosing Magento versions, there are two types- Magento Community Edition and Magento Enterprise Edition. 

The community edition is the free edition that anyone can use to create a marketplace with Magento. It needs zero investment, which is why it has become quite popular among eCommerce startups. 

Magento Enterprise Edition or Magento Commerce is the paid version perfect for big and medium marketplace development. So which one to choose? Which one will be perfect for you? Let’s find out. Here are some major comparisons between the two editions to understand which one you will need for your business. 

Security Requirements

If you need a robust security system backing your eCommerce website, then choosing the Magento Enterprise solution would be a better idea for you. When it comes to security, the enterprise edition is far ahead of the free edition. With Magento enterprise edition you will get access to secure payment gateways, regular scanning for any kind of security breaches, external penetration testings, and many other security supports. 

Performance Quality

If your eCommerce website is a small one with fewer resources on it, choosing the Community version would be better for you, as it provides faster performance to small marketplace websites. And if you are still in need of better functionalities, then you can just add the third party Magento marketplace extensions to your website. 

On the other hand, if your website happens to be a bigger one with numerous resources and handling a lot of orders and traffic, choosing the Magento Enterprise edition would be a better decision.
