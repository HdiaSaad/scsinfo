Agile website redevelopment and redesign for a Leading Wine Distribution Company in the US, bumping up conversions by 30%

About

Wineshipping is one of the largest technology-enabled, alcohol logistics networks in the US, with revenues of over $10 million and 15,000 monthly visitors. They have an expansive network to deliver wine, beer, and other alcoholic beverages, over several major locations.

Services

  • Consulting & Planning
  • Information Architecture
  • UX/UI Design
  • Web Development
  • Ongoing Maintenance
visit website

Background

Wineshipping, America’s largest wine distribution organization, reached out to us to improve lead generation and the on-site sales validation process. They wanted a fresh, interactive and lively feel for the whole website. Wineshipping wanted us to deliver a unique website that not only attracts users and guides them throughout their user journey, but also converts and leaves the user with a fresh memory. We kickstarted the process with a strategy that would help our client accomplish their goals in the liquor industry and strengthen their standing in the competitive market.

The Challenge

Since the current website had issues acquiring and maintaining leads, the goal was to develop an optimized website that appealed to users, increased engagement and boosted conversion rates. The primary focus was on the UI and UX of the website, to maximize the user’s journey and experience.

The Approach

To fulfill our client’s goals, we approached the challenges based on the data derived from our competitor analysis, identifying the target market and defining the user journey with a storytelling approach. We used different marketing tools such as SEMRush, Moz and Google Analytics to understand the user’s needs and loopholes. We applied the industry best-practices and delved deeper into their SWOT Analysis, Empathy Mapping and Information Architecture (IA) of the website along with defining the UI guideline. We moved on to documenting the hand-drawn sketches and high-fidelity wireframes for the website.

The Solution

Through data analytics and some intel from our client, we were able to determine Wineshipping’s competition in the market. Our focus was to create a website that features a top notch UX/UI and acts as an efficient sales validator. Subsequently, we defined the information architecture and identified the elements of the website. Our in-house information architecture experts employed the card sorting process to label elements and meet the business objectives, completing the sales funnel in the process. The research on the end-users gave us ample ideas about the kind of intuitive design the target demographic desires. We designed and developed a website that stood out from the company’s top five competitors. It featured a strong brand messaging and an intuitive design that aligned with Wineshipping’s brand identity.
